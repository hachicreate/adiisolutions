Privacy Policy

Last updated: November 25, 2025

　

hachicreate (hereinafter referred to as "the Provider") establishes this Privacy Policy (hereinafter referred to as "this Policy") regarding the handling of users' personal information in the smartphone application (hereinafter referred to as "this App") the Provider provides. This Policy sets forth the personal information the Provider acquires, the purposes of its use, and the Provider's management methods, to ensure that users can use this App with confidence.
　

Article 1 (Personal Information)

"Personal Information" as referred to in the Personal Information Protection Act, means information about a living individual that can identify the specific individual by name, date of birth, address, telephone number, contact information, etc., or information that can identify an individual solely by itself (Personal Identification Information), such as facial features, fingerprints, voiceprints, or the insurer number on a health insurance card.

Article 2 (Method of Collecting Personal Information)

The Provider primarily collects users' personal information through the following methods:

  • Direct Provision from the User: Users may provide information such as their name, date of birth, address, telephone number, and email address when registering to use this App, making inquiries, or answering surveys.
  • Collection from Partners: The Provider may collect transaction records, payment-related information, and other information including the user's personal information from the Provider's partners (including information providers, advertisers, and ad distributors; hereinafter referred to as "Partners").
  • Automatic Collection by App Functions: the Provider may automatically collect information such as IP addresses, device information, OS information, and information about the usage status of this App, to the extent necessary for providing this App.

Furthermore, this App uses the Google AdMob advertising distribution service, which may allow Google and other third parties to acquire users' device information, advertising IDs, etc., and use them for ad personalization and optimization of distribution. For more details, please check Google's Privacy Policy.

Article 3 (Purpose of Collecting and Using Personal Information)

The main purposes for which the Provider collects and uses users' personal information are as follows:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve this App.
  • To respond to users' questions and inquiries (including identity verification).
  • To send notifications about new features, updates, campaigns, and other services provided by the Provider (via email, push notifications, etc.).
  • To send notifications regarding maintenance, system failures, and important announcements for this App.
  • To prevent acts that violate the terms of service or fraudulent acts, and to identify and restrict the use of this App by such users.
  • To allow users to confirm, change, or delete their registration information and usage history.
  • To bill users who use paid services.
  • To perform operations related to the above purposes of use.
  • Analysis for the improvement of services and the development of new services.

Article 4 (Change of Purpose of Use)

The Provider shall change the purpose of use of personal information only when it is reasonably recognized that the purpose of use after the change is related to the purpose of use before the change. If the purpose of use is changed, the Provider shall post the changed purpose on this App or the Provider's website.

Article 5 (Provision of Personal Information to a Third Party)

The Provider will not provide personal information to a third party without obtaining the user's prior consent, except in the cases listed below. However, this excludes cases permitted by the Personal Information Protection Act and other laws and regulations.

  1. When it is necessary for the protection of a person's life, body, or property, and it is difficult to obtain the consent of the individual.
  2. When it is particularly necessary for the improvement of public health or the sound upbringing of children, and it is difficult to obtain the consent of the individual.
  3. When it is necessary to cooperate with a national agency, a local government, or an individual entrusted by them in executing the duties prescribed by laws and regulations, and obtaining the consent of the individual is likely to impede the execution of those duties.
  4. When the Provider has notified or publicly announced the following matters in advance, and has filed a notification with the Personal Information Protection Commission:
    • That the purpose of use includes provision to a third party.
    • The items of data to be provided to a third party.
    • The means or method of provision to a third party.
    • To cease the provision of personal information to a third party at the request of the individual.
    • The method for accepting the individual's request.

Notwithstanding the provisions of the preceding paragraph, in the following cases, the recipient of the information shall not be considered a third party:

  1. When the Provider entrusts all or part of the handling of personal information to a third party within the scope necessary to achieve the purpose of use.
  2. When personal information is provided due to the succession of business through merger or other reasons.
  3. When personal information is jointly used with a specific person, and the individual is notified in advance or is placed in a state where they can easily know the fact, the items of personal information to be jointly used, the scope of the joint users, the purpose of use by the user, and the name of the person responsible for the management of the personal information.

Article 6 (Disclosure of Personal Information)

When the Provider is requested by the individual to disclose their personal information, the Provider shall disclose it to the individual without delay. However, the Provider may decide not to disclose all or part of the information if disclosure falls under any of the following, and if the Provider decides not to disclose, the Provider shall notify the individual without delay:

  1. When there is a risk of harming the life, body, property, or other rights and interests of the individual or a third party.
  2. When there is a risk of significantly hindering the proper execution of the Provider's business.
  3. When it would violate other laws and regulations.

Notwithstanding the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the Provider will not, in principle, disclose information other than personal information, such as usage history and characteristic information.

Article 7 (Correction and Deletion of Personal Information)

A user may request the Provider to correct, add, or delete (hereinafter referred to as "Correction, etc.") their personal information held by the Provider if they believe the information is incorrect, according to the procedures the Provider prescribes.

If the Provider deems it necessary to respond to the user's request after receiving the request in the preceding paragraph, the Provider shall conduct the Correction, etc. of the relevant personal information without delay.

If the Provider makes a Correction, etc. based on the provisions of the preceding paragraph, or if the Provider decides not to make a Correction, etc., the Provider shall notify the user of this without delay.

Article 8 (Suspension of Use, etc. of Personal Information)

If the Provider is requested by the individual to suspend the use or delete (hereinafter referred to as "Suspension of Use, etc.") of their personal information on the grounds that it is handled beyond the scope of the purpose of use, or that it was acquired by fraudulent means, the Provider shall conduct the necessary investigation without delay.

Based on the results of the investigation in the preceding paragraph, if the Provider determine that it is necessary to comply with the request, the Provider shall implement the Suspension of Use, etc. of the relevant personal information without delay.

If the Provider implements the Suspension of Use, etc. based on the provisions of the preceding paragraph, or if the Provider decides not to implement the Suspension of Use, etc., the Provider shall notify the user of this without delay.

Notwithstanding the preceding two paragraphs, if the Suspension of Use, etc. requires a large amount of expense, or if it is otherwise difficult to implement the Suspension of Use, etc., but the Provider can take alternative measures necessary to protect the user's rights and interests, the Provider shall take this alternative measure.

Article 9 (Compliance with International Laws)

In addition to complying with Japan’s Personal Information Protection Act, the Provider will also take into account, as necessary, the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act (CCPA/CPRA), the Personal Information Protection Law of China (PIPL), and other relevant laws and regulations of each country.

Article 10 (Cross-Border Data Transfer)

The Provider may store or transfer users’ personal information to servers located inside or outside Japan. In such cases, the Provider will take necessary and appropriate measures to protect users’ rights and interests in accordance with applicable laws.

Article 11 (Protection of Minors)

For users under the age of 13, the Provider will only collect and use personal information with the consent of a parent or legal guardian, and will handle such information with special care.

Article 12 (Changes to the Privacy Policy)

The Provider may change the content of this Policy without prior notice if required by changes in laws and regulations or when the Provider deems it necessary. The changed Policy shall be notified to users by posting it within this App or on the Provider's website, or by other appropriate methods, and shall become effective after a certain period has elapsed after notification.
